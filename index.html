Uniqu - A Pop-Up Fashion Brand

Adrienne Mary

A fashion brand that is about the story, the process, and the people involved.

We are a mobile brand that discovers, shares, and celebrates creative communities around the nation, by designing and producing a pop-up fashion collection inspired by the unique qualities of each location we visit; encouraging the start-up of independent fashion labels by local designers, through our collaboration with a local shop that will allow us to display our design process and sell our final product to the local community.

Adrienne discovered graphic design in high school, fell in love, and since her childhood dreams of being a mermaid didn't happen, graphic design was the next best thing. Adrienne also has an obsession with the Disney princesses, hence the desire to be a mermaid, and also for neon colors.

Alyssa Hans

Helping children learn care routines with a companion app for pet foster programs.

Children often want pets, but are turned off by the responsibilities. This mobile task management app helps kids age 9 to 12 track pet care chores and learn their animal’s daily needs. The app complements pet foster programs lasting a fixed period of time. After completing the program, the parents receive the child’s results, opening opportunities for discussion on the family’s pet readiness.

Word Building

Andrea Schwallie

Teaching Latin roots and affixes to students in grades 4 through 6

A large number of English words are derived from Latin, but it isn’t generally taught as a language until high school. By introducing common prefixes, bases, and suffixes to younger students, they can see how words are put together and how larger words can be broken down into their fundamental components. This educational card game, intended for students in grades 4-6, helps children identify two part and three part English words that stem from Latin.

A fascination with color theory—along with a small collection of product labels and magazine ads—originally compelled Andrea to study graphic design. Her time in DAAP has been an excellent learning experience and she is grateful for the encouragement she has received from family, friends, and faculty members along the way.

Ohio State Parks

Ann Burger

Comprehensive trail wayfinding system + interactive app for the Ohio State Parks

The Ohio State Parks are a wonderful resource to connect visitors and locals to the beauty of the outdoors. While the Parks themselves display the natural wonders of the state, the signs used to communicate to visitors and guide them through the park are outdated and inconsistent. The new system of signs would combine interactive elements with traditional static signs and would allow hikers to clearly identify trails and pertinent information, providing a better, more effective experience to the user.

act: autism communication tool

Annie Davis & Sarah Weinstein

Encouraging classroom communication through interaction.

One of the greatest contributors to meltdowns and behavioral outbursts in the classroom for children with Autism is their inability to verbally communicate their wants and needs. What if classroom communication between students with Autism and their teachers could be improved, meltdowns could be prevented, and verbal communication could be encouraged with an interactive tool? Our iPad application promotes success in education by allowing children with Autism to verbally communicate their wants and needs through imagery and sound.

Being visual and kinesthetic learners themselves, Annie and Sarah both entered DAAP with enthusiasm for interactive media and a desire to use the visual world as communicative art. Their combined passions for design research, interactive design, user experience design, and problem solving have guided them through many rewarding experiences.

Rebranding Banned Book Week

Arielle Selzer

Banned Book Week raises awareness to the practice of book banning within the United States.

Promoted by several groups, the campaign is often unorganized and without a unified identity. The rebrand of the Banned Book Week will focus on creating a unified national identity and messaging standard to be used across the country. The new message will focus, not only on raising awareness of the practice, on emphasizing the negative effects of censorship.

Arielle became interested in design during high school. Through co-op she has worked in both large and small design firms, and developed skills in both print and digital media. When not designing, she can be found listening to music, reading, or drinking tea.

Connected Comics

Bob Kelley

Make new friends and share comics in the digital age.

Brick and mortar comic shops have represented a safe place for comic book conversation and trading since their inception. Connected Comics is a tablet application dedicated to recreating that experience for the digital generation. My goal is to use local network connections to encourage discussion, trading, and recreate that experience on the go.

Bob grew up with an interest in comic books, videogames, and heavy metal. From a young age he found an interest in emulating the lessons these media provided, and design school was the logical next step. These days the majority of his time is spent finding creative ways to procrastinate.

Type 1 Manager

Christie Gettler

Managing Type I Diabetes is important, but life can get in the way. Type I Manager can help to make treatment safer, easier, and more effective.

Managing Type I Diabetes can be a lot of work - remembering to test blood glucose 4-10 times a day, counting every carb and mintue of exercise, calculating and administering insulin doses - it can be overwhelming. Type I Manager is a mobile application for individuals with Type I where they can set personal reminders to test their blood glucose, change their infusion sets, and reorder supplies, help them calculate proper insulin doses, and track patterns in their blood glucose, insulin, and activity levels. The app also has a safety feature that will notify an emergency contact with a text message and the individuals GPS location should they experience a diabetic crisis.

Christie is a Cincinnati native that has recently fallen in love with traveling. When not designing, nothing beats a good book, stand-up comedy, or play-off hockey.
