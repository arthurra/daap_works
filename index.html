Type 1 Manager

Christie Gettler

Managing Type I Diabetes is important, but life can get in the way. Type I Manager can help to make treatment safer, easier, and more effective.

Managing Type I Diabetes can be a lot of work - remembering to test blood glucose 4-10 times a day, counting every carb and mintue of exercise, calculating and administering insulin doses - it can be overwhelming. Type I Manager is a mobile application for individuals with Type I where they can set personal reminders to test their blood glucose, change their infusion sets, and reorder supplies, help them calculate proper insulin doses, and track patterns in their blood glucose, insulin, and activity levels. The app also has a safety feature that will notify an emergency contact with a text message and the individuals GPS location should they experience a diabetic crisis.

Redesigning the US Electoral Ballot

Nick Ross

Through standardization, the United States electoral ballot can become a more useful tool for voters.

The current system for ballot design in the United States creates ballots riddled with poor visual communication, leading to voter errors, causing hundreds of thousands of votes to be tossed out each election. In addition to this, each county has its own ballot layout, further complicating tallying the votes. Developing a national standard for ballot design will bring America to the forefront in worldwide democracy, and a designerly take on the layout will eliminate the visual communication currently plaguing the system.

Don't Feed The Deer

Peter Kemme

Educating people on the problems that are excacerbated by feeding deer in suburban/urban areas.

In Ohio and the neighboring states, there is a high-population of deer that causes problems for people living there. Car accidents, tick-borne diseases, and property damage are three of the biggest problems caused by deer and are worsened by people putting out food for them on their property. “Don’t Feed the Deer” is a series of PSAs to educate people on the problems associated with the backyard feeding of deer.

Peter started his collegiate career as a pirate and has since settled on being a lumberjack, a lumberjack who enjoys animation and comedy. He also has a penchant for comics, video games, and playing bass. He sat a foot away from Amy Poehler once. That was awesome.

What Now?

Rebecca Katz

A Guide to Success After College Graduation

When it comes to graduating college, assuming all of the financial responsibilities many students were previously unaware of can be daunting and overwhelming. There are millions of books out there that address the topic of financing, but all of them are text heavy and not very user friendly. I am designing a book that explains these topics in a visually exciting way with pages for interaction, making the book completely personalizable, portable, and easy to understand.

Blueprint

Robin Anderson

Exposing high school students to design through a traveling exhibit.

Most high school students are unaware and unexposed to what design is and how it plays a part in our daily lives. Through a exhibit that will travel from various high schools, students will be educated about design on a basic level, and shown that it is a viable career path. The exhibit includes testimonials from various designers, shows the wide scope of design and demonstrates that design is creative problem solving; not art.

I'm Not a Feminist... But

Sara Baumgartner

Feminism is often misunderstood and diminished. Young women should feel comfortable standing up for their rights.

Most miss the point of what feminism is really about; the idea that men and women are equal. There are many women who are afraid to confirm that they are feminists simply because of stereotypes, but this video is meant to help them feel more comfortable in daily situations explaining to people that feminism isn’t a dirty word. Viewers will be prompted to learn more about the topic of feminism and share the video with friends.

Redesigning a Church

Sarah Fukatsu

A church may be a way of life, but it also functions on a similar level as an organzation. Therefore, it also needs a strong identity to move forward and grow.

The Unification Church has existed for years, and has a dominant symbol it has used, but overall, has lacked a focus in what its trying to convey. It's a way of life, therefore it has that advantage, but in order to maintain its members, and continue growth, the church needs a rebrand, and a redesign of the identity. I am creating a messaging strategy, and a visual language to convey the values of the faith that will speak to the members.

Bridging the Gap

Sarah Rolitsky

Improving the communication gap within the developmentally disabled community.

Bridging the Gap is a graphic system aimed toward improving communication between non-verbal, developmentally disabled users and their care givers. This system includes emotion, daily actities, and task analysis graphics, as well as a travel-friendly, display board. Bridging the Gap gives communication boards a standardized, well-designed look, where "makeshift" boards have failed in the past.

Five years later and a whole lot wiser, she is ready to head back to her new job in Cleveland, where she will continue her graphic design career.

Ski Rental Process

Suzanne Herbert & Jen Kowal

A redesign of the ski rental process at Perfect North Slopes.

The ski rental process is lengthy and confusing, especially to new skiers who don't have a working knowledge of how their equipment will affect their personal skiing experience. We hope to improve this process by designing and building an iPad kiosk app and creating new way-finding signage for inside the resort.
